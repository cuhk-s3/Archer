## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: Contextual Same Sign Violation

- **Target**: A loop with an induction variable comparison where the operands have the same sign at the common dominator of the users, but not at the comparison itself.
- **Rationale**: The fix uses `SE->haveSameSignAt(S, X, CtxI)` to determine if the operands have the same sign in the context of `CtxI`. However, `CtxI` is the common dominator of all users of the comparison, not the comparison itself. If the comparison is executed in a context where the operands do not have the same sign, but they do at `CtxI`, this could lead to incorrect optimization.
- **Expected Issue**: The comparison might be incorrectly marked as `samesign` and canonicalized to an unsigned predicate, leading to incorrect evaluation if the operands do not actually have the same sign at the point of the comparison.
