## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: PRE of llvm.protected.field.ptr

- **Target**: A test case with a diamond control flow where `llvm.protected.field.ptr` is used in the join block or one of the branches, making it a candidate for PRE.
- **Rationale**: The fix prevents PRE (Partial Redundancy Elimination) of `llvm.protected.field.ptr` intrinsics. If we remove the fix, GVN will perform PRE on this intrinsic, moving it to a predecessor block and inserting a PHI node. This violates the requirement that the intrinsic should be paired with its load/store to avoid unnecessary address escapes.
- **Expected Issue**: The `llvm.protected.field.ptr` intrinsic will be hoisted to a predecessor block, causing the pointer to escape its intended scope and potentially breaking the structure protection mechanism.

The fix correctly prevents GVN from performing Partial Redundancy Elimination (PRE) on the `llvm.protected.field.ptr` intrinsic.
Without the fix, GVN would hoist the intrinsic from the merge block (`bb3`) to the predecessor block (`bb1`) and insert a PHI node in `bb3`. This would cause the pointer to escape its intended scope, breaking the structure protection mechanism.
The provided test case demonstrates this scenario with a diamond control flow where the intrinsic is used in the join block (`bb3`) and one of the branches (`bb1` and `bb2`).
The `trans` tool confirmed that the intrinsic is preserved in the optimized IR with the fix applied.
The `difftest` tool crashed with `lli` because `lli` does not support the `llvm.protected.field.ptr` intrinsic (it is target-specific to AArch64).
The `verify` tool failed to prove the transformation because `alive2` does not support the `llvm.protected.field.ptr` intrinsic.
However, the `trans` tool output is sufficient to verify that the fix works as expected.
