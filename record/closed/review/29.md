### Strategy 1: FDiv by Negative Zero

- **Target**: A test case with `fdiv` where LHS is known positive and RHS is known to be `-0.0` or can be `-0.0` (but not negative finite/inf).
- **Rationale**: The patch assumes that if both LHS and RHS are known never to be negative, the result of `fdiv` is known never to be negative. However, if LHS is a positive number and RHS is `-0.0` (which is not considered `fcNegative` but `fcNegZero`), the result is `-Inf`, which is negative. The patch checks `KnownRHS.isKnownNever(fcNegative)` but `fcNegative` only covers `fcNegFinite | fcNegInf`, not `fcNegZero`. Therefore, `+X / -0.0` produces `-Inf` (which is `fcNegative`), breaking the assumption.
- **Expected Issue**: The compiler will incorrectly deduce that the result of `fdiv` cannot be negative, leading to incorrect optimizations (e.g., replacing a check for negative with false).
