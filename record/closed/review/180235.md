## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: Test CSE after SimplifyRecipes

- **Target**: VPlan CSE and SimplifyRecipes interaction
- **Rationale**: The patch adds a call to `simplifyRecipes` before `cse`. This means that `cse` will now operate on simplified recipes. We should test if this new order exposes any issues where `simplifyRecipes` produces recipes that `cse` incorrectly handles or fails to handle.
- **Expected Issue**: The compiler might crash or generate incorrect code because CSE replaces a recipe with another one that dominates it, but the replacement recipe might not have been simplified yet, or the simplified recipe might not be CSE'd if it was simplified after CSE. By simplifying before CSE, we ensure that CSE operates on the simplified recipes, potentially finding more redundancies. However, if simplifyRecipes creates new recipes or modifies existing ones in a way that breaks CSE's assumptions (e.g., by changing the order of operands or creating new instructions that are not properly handled by CSE), it could lead to missed CSE opportunities or incorrect replacements.
