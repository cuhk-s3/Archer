## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: Callsite with `readnone` attribute

- **Target**: A test with `sqrt` call where the callsite has `readnone` but the declaration does not.
- **Rationale**: To verify that callsite attributes are preserved and propagated to the new `sqrtf` call when the declaration does not have them.
- **Expected Issue**: Without the fix, the `readnone` attribute would be lost on the new `sqrtf` call, potentially inhibiting further optimizations.

### Strategy 2: Callee with `denormal-fp-math` attribute

- **Target**: A test with `sqrt` call where the `sqrt` declaration has `denormal-fp-math` attribute but the callsite does not.
- **Rationale**: To verify that callee-specific attributes that are illegal or inappropriate for callsites are not propagated.
- **Expected Issue**: Without the fix, the `denormal-fp-math` attribute would be incorrectly applied to the new `sqrtf` callsite, potentially causing a verifier error or incorrect semantics.

### Strategy 3: Callsite with `strictfp` attribute

- **Target**: A test with `sqrt` call where the callsite has `strictfp` attribute.
- **Rationale**: To ensure that strict floating-point semantics are preserved when shrinking the math function.
- **Expected Issue**: Without the fix, the `strictfp` attribute would be lost, altering the floating-point semantics of the program.

### Strategy 4: Callsite with `memory(none)` attribute

- **Target**: A test with `sqrt` call where the callsite has `memory(none)` attribute.
- **Rationale**: To verify that modern memory attributes on the callsite are correctly propagated to the new call.
- **Expected Issue**: Without the fix, the `memory(none)` attribute would be lost, potentially inhibiting further optimizations.

### Strategy 5: Callsite with `alwaysinline` attribute

- **Target**: A test with `sqrt` call where the callsite has `alwaysinline` attribute.
- **Rationale**: To verify that inlining directives on the callsite are preserved.
- **Expected Issue**: Without the fix, the `alwaysinline` attribute would be lost, changing the inlining behavior of the new call.
