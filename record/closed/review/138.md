## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: Incorrect FMF Inference on Rounding Intrinsics

- **Target**: Rounding intrinsics (trunc, floor, ceil, rint, nearbyint, round, roundeven) with conditionally executed source operands or context-dependent known classes.
- **Rationale**: The patch infers fast math flags (FMF) for rounding intrinsics (trunc, floor, ceil, rint, nearbyint, round, roundeven) based on the known floating-point classes of the source operand. If the source operand is conditionally executed or its known classes are context-dependent, inferring FMF globally for the rounding intrinsic might be incorrect.
- **Expected Issue**: The fast math flags on the rounding intrinsic might be incorrectly inferred, leading to miscompilations if the inferred flags are invalid for the actual execution path.

### Strategy 2: Context Leakage in Multi-Use Rounding Intrinsics

- **Target**: Rounding intrinsics with source operands that have multiple uses and context-dependent known classes.
- **Rationale**: The patch infers FMF for rounding intrinsics based on the known classes of the source operand. If the source operand has multiple uses and its known classes are context-dependent (e.g., due to a dominating condition), inferring FMF globally for the rounding intrinsic might leak context-specific assumptions to other uses.
- **Expected Issue**: The rounding intrinsic might be incorrectly simplified or its FMF incorrectly inferred if the source operand has multiple uses and its known classes are context-dependent.

### Strategy 3: Mishandling of Vector Rounding Intrinsics

- **Target**: Vector rounding intrinsics with source operands that have lane-crossing dependencies or poison/undef elements.
- **Rationale**: The patch infers FMF for rounding intrinsics based on the known classes of the source operand. If the source operand is a vector with lane-crossing dependencies or poison/undef elements, the known classes might not be valid for all lanes, leading to incorrect FMF inference.
- **Expected Issue**: The rounding intrinsic might be incorrectly simplified or its FMF incorrectly inferred if the source operand is a vector with lane-crossing dependencies or poison/undef elements.

### Strategy 4: Mishandling of NaN Sign Bits and Payloads in Rounding Intrinsics

- **Target**: Rounding intrinsics with NaN source operands that have specific sign bits or payloads.
- **Rationale**: The patch infers FMF for rounding intrinsics based on the known classes of the source operand. If the source operand is a NaN with a specific sign bit or payload, the inferred FMF might incorrectly assume no NaNs or overwrite the NaN sign bit/payload.
- **Expected Issue**: The rounding intrinsic might be incorrectly simplified or its FMF incorrectly inferred if the source operand is a NaN with a specific sign bit or payload.

### Strategy 5: Mishandling of Denormal Values in Rounding Intrinsics

- **Target**: Rounding intrinsics with denormal source operands and non-IEEE denormal modes.
- **Rationale**: The patch infers FMF for rounding intrinsics based on the known classes of the source operand. If the source operand is a denormal value and the denormal mode is not IEEE, the known classes might not accurately reflect the actual value, leading to incorrect FMF inference.
- **Expected Issue**: The rounding intrinsic might be incorrectly simplified or its FMF incorrectly inferred if the source operand is a denormal value and the denormal mode is not IEEE.
