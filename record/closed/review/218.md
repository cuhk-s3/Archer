The fix correctly addresses the issue where `VectorCombine` would crash when trying to fold a vector reduction and a comparison on an `i1` vector. The crash was caused by an out-of-bounds bit access in `APInt::operator[]` when evaluating the second bit of a 1-bit value. The fix adds an early return for `i1` vectors, correctly identifying the equivalence between `and` and `umin`, and `or` and `umax` for `i1` vectors without accessing out-of-bounds bits.

I have tested the fix with various combinations of vector reductions (`and`, `or`, `umin`, `umax`) on `i1` vectors, including both fixed-length and scalable vectors, and different comparison predicates (`eq`, `ne`) and constants (`false`, `true`). The tests confirm that the fix prevents the crash and correctly folds the operations. No new bugs were found.
