## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: Missing dropUBImplyingAttrsAndMetadata on copysign simplification

- **Target**: A test case with `copysign` and UB-implying attributes like `noundef` or `range` where the sign bit is known.
- **Rationale**: The patch adds `CI->dropUBImplyingAttrsAndMetadata()` when simplifying `copysign` to `0.0` or `-1.0` based on the known sign bit. This indicates that before the patch, these attributes were not dropped, which could lead to UB if the simplified instruction retains attributes that are no longer valid.
- **Expected Issue**: The `copysign` instruction might be incorrectly simplified to `0.0` or `-1.0` (or similar constants) while retaining UB-implying attributes like `noundef` or `range` on the call instruction, leading to undefined behavior if the original instruction was conditionally executed or if the simplification is invalid in some contexts.

### Strategy 2: Missing dropUBImplyingAttrsAndMetadata on copysign simplification (fcPositive/fcNegative)

- **Target**: A test case with `copysign` and UB-implying attributes where the demanded mask is `fcNegative` or `fcPositive`.
- **Rationale**: The patch adds `CI->dropUBImplyingAttrsAndMetadata()` when simplifying `copysign` based on `KnownSign.SignBit`. However, it does NOT add it when simplifying based on `DemandedMask & fcNegative` or `DemandedMask & fcPositive`. This might be a missed case in the patch itself, where UB-implying attributes should also be dropped when simplifying to `0.0` or `-1.0` based on the demanded mask.
- **Expected Issue**: The `copysign` instruction might be incorrectly simplified to `0.0` or `-1.0` while retaining UB-implying attributes, leading to undefined behavior.

### Strategy 3: Missed simplification when sign bits are known and equal

- **Target**: A test case with `copysign(x, y)` where both `x` and `y` have known and equal sign bits (e.g., both are known positive or both are known negative).
- **Rationale**: The patch adds a check `if (Known.SignBit && KnownSign.SignBit && *Known.SignBit == *KnownSign.SignBit) return CI->getOperand(0);`. This means that before the patch, `copysign(x, y)` was not simplified to `x` even if both `x` and `y` were known to have the same sign bit.
- **Expected Issue**: The `copysign` instruction might not be simplified to its first operand when the sign bits of both operands are known and equal.

### Strategy 4: Missed simplification when sign bit of second operand is known

- **Target**: A test case with `copysign(x, y)` where the sign bit of `y` is known (e.g., `y` is a positive constant or a known positive value).
- **Rationale**: The patch adds checks `if (KnownSign.SignBit == false)` and `if (KnownSign.SignBit == true)` to simplify `copysign` to `0.0` or `-1.0` respectively. This means that before the patch, this simplification was not performed based on the known sign bit of the second operand.
- **Expected Issue**: The `copysign` instruction might not be simplified to `0.0` or `-1.0` when the sign bit of the second operand is known.

### Strategy 5: Missed simplification with nnan flag

- **Target**: A test case with `copysign(x, y)` and the `nnan` flag, where `y` is known to be positive or negative except for the possibility of being NaN.
- **Rationale**: The patch adds `if (FMF.noNaNs()) KnownSign.knownNot(fcNan);`. This allows the known sign bit to be determined more accurately when the `nnan` flag is present, enabling further simplifications. Before the patch, the `nnan` flag was not used to refine the known FP class of the second operand.
- **Expected Issue**: The `copysign` instruction might not be simplified correctly when the `nnan` fast-math flag is present.
