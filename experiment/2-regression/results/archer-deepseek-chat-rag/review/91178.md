## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: PoisonChainPropagation

- **Target**: Create chains of instructions where poison propagates through recursive replacement in unexpected ways
- **Rationale**: impliesPoison(BO, Op) may not account for poison created in intermediate instructions during recursive replacement
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect poison safety assumption leading to miscompilation

### Strategy 2: VectorCrossLaneOps

- **Target**: Test vector operations that could be cross-lane but aren't in the exclusion list
- **Rationale**: The fix only excludes ShuffleVectorInst, CallBase, BitCastInst, but other instructions could also be cross-lane
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect vector simplification violating per-lane semantics

### Strategy 3: DeepRecursionTypeSafety

- **Target**: Create deep instruction chains with type conversions during replacement
- **Rationale**: Recursive replacement through type-changing instructions could create type mismatches
- **Expected Issue**: Type safety violations or incorrect simplifications

### Strategy 4: IndirectAbsorberPropagation

- **Target**: Create patterns where absorber constant appears indirectly through multiple replacement steps
- **Rationale**: The new check (NewOps[0] == Absorber || NewOps[1] == Absorber) may miss cases where absorber appears through complex transformations
- **Expected Issue**: Missed optimization opportunities or incorrect simplifications

### Strategy 5: SelfSimplifyCycles

- **Target**: Create patterns where recursive replacement creates simplification cycles
- **Rationale**: The PreventSelfSimplify logic may not handle cycles created through recursive replacement
- **Expected Issue**: Infinite loops or incorrect nullptr returns
