## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: Incorrect propagation of `inbounds` to `nsw` for multiple GEPs

- **Target**: A test case with a chain of `inbounds` GEPs where the intermediate offset addition overflows signedly, but the final pointer is still in bounds.
- **Rationale**: The patch computes the total offset of a chain of GEPs by adding their individual offsets. It propagates the `inbounds` flag of the GEPs to the `nsw` flag of the `add` instruction. However, `inbounds` on multiple GEPs only guarantees that the final pointer is in bounds of the allocated object, not that the intermediate additions of offsets do not overflow signedly. For example, if we have `gep inbounds (gep inbounds Ptr, A), B`, the total offset is `A + B`. Even if both GEPs are `inbounds`, `A + B` can overflow signedly if `A` and `B` have different signs and their sum exceeds the signed maximum value. By propagating `inbounds` to `nsw`, the compiler might incorrectly assume that `A + B` does not overflow signedly, leading to incorrect optimizations.
- **Expected Issue**: The `icmp` instruction will be incorrectly folded to `true` or `false` because the `nsw` flag is incorrectly propagated to the `add` instruction, leading to a poison value when the offset addition overflows signedly.
