## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: NaN Sign Bit Canonicalization

- **Target**: Test operations that might canonicalize NaNs (canonicalize, sqrt, etc.)
- **Rationale**: The fix assumes NaN sign bits are preserved, but some operations may clear them, leading to incorrect sign inference for NaN results.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect sign inference for NaN results from canonicalizing operations.

### Strategy 2: Vector Element-Wise Sign Inconsistency

- **Target**: Test vectors with mixed signs and vector operations
- **Rationale**: Current implementation sets SignBit to nullopt for mixed signs, but element-wise analysis might be needed for vector operations like shuffles or extracts.
- **Expected Issue**: Missing optimization opportunities or incorrect transformations for vectors with mixed signs.

### Strategy 3: NSZ Flag Sign Inference

- **Target**: Test operations with nsz flag and signed zeros
- **Rationale**: The fix might miss sign optimizations enabled by nsz flag, particularly for -0.0/+0.0 cases where nsz guarantees sign bit 0.
- **Expected Issue**: Missing sign bit inference for -0.0/+0.0 cases with nsz flag.

### Strategy 4: Deep Sign Inference Chains

- **Target**: Test nested floating-point operations with sign dependencies
- **Rationale**: Sign inference might not compose correctly through operation chains, especially with depth limitations or complex expression patterns.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect sign inference for complex nested expressions.

### Strategy 5: Denormal-to-Zero Sign Preservation

- **Target**: Test with different denormal modes (DAZ, FTZ)
- **Rationale**: Sign of flushed denormals might not be handled correctly, particularly with preserve-sign modes that could affect sign inference.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect sign inference when denormals flush to zero with sign preservation.
