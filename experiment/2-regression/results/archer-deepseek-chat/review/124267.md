## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: Vector samesign with poison elements

- **Target**: Test vectors with samesign predicates and constants containing poison elements
- **Rationale**: The m_NonPositive/m_NonNegative matchers accept vectors with undefined elements, which combined with samesign semantics could lead to incorrect poison handling
- **Expected Issue**: Optimization incorrectly applies when vector elements are poison, leading to miscompilation

### Strategy 2: Mismatched samesign predicate combinations

- **Target**: Test all combinations of samesign predicates with their signed/unsigned counterparts
- **Rationale**: CmpPredicate::getMatching might not handle all edge cases correctly, especially with flipped signedness
- **Expected Issue**: getMatching returns incorrect matching for some samesign predicate combinations

### Strategy 3: NSW flag analysis edge cases

- **Target**: Test cases where subtraction appears to have NSW but actually doesn't due to value ranges
- **Rationale**: The optimization relies on nsw flag being correct; incorrect NSW inference could lead to wrong results
- **Expected Issue**: Optimization fires on subtraction without true NSW guarantee

### Strategy 4: Poison propagation through samesign icmp

- **Target**: Test cases where samesign icmp produces poison due to different signs
- **Rationale**: The optimization might incorrectly assume the samesign icmp is non-poison when analyzing implications
- **Expected Issue**: Optimization assumes non-poison condition when poison is actually possible

### Strategy 5: Recursive implication with samesign in isImpliedCondMatchingOperands

- **Target**: Test the interaction between samesign predicates in the matching operands check
- **Rationale**: The isImpliedCondMatchingOperands(LPred, RPred) == false/true check might not handle samesign correctly
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect true/false return from the optimization due to samesign predicate mismatch
