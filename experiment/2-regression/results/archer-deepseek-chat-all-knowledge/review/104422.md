## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: ComplexBitPatternTruncation

- **Target**: Values with complex bit patterns that fool MaskedValueIsZero/ComputeNumSignBits
- **Rationale**: The IsPotentiallyTruncated function relies on MaskedValueIsZero and ComputeNumSignBits which may give incorrect answers for values with complex arithmetic, poison bits, or unusual bit patterns. This could lead to incorrect truncation and loss of data.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect truncation leading to wrong computation results or poison propagation.

### Strategy 2: MultiUseProfitabilityConflict

- **Target**: Values used in multiple places with conflicting profitability requirements
- **Rationale**: A scalar value may be used in multiple vector trees with different bitwidth requirements. The current analysis may not properly handle cases where demoting in one subtree breaks another subtree.
- **Expected Issue**: Value demoted to wrong bitwidth causing incorrect results in some uses.

### Strategy 3: ReductionBitwidthMismatch

- **Target**: Reduction trees with complex bitwidth requirements
- **Rationale**: ReductionBitWidth calculation analyzes reduction users but may miss complex patterns or interactions between multiple reductions. Incorrect reduction bitwidth could cause overflow or precision loss.
- **Expected Issue**: Reduction overflow or incorrect results due to wrong bitwidth selection.

### Strategy 4: SignednessPropagationError

- **Target**: Mixed signed/unsigned operations across subtrees
- **Rationale**: The patch tracks signedness per TreeEntry but may incorrectly propagate signedness across casts or when mixing signed/unsigned operations. This could lead to incorrect sext/zext selection.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect sign extension causing wrong values or poison.

### Strategy 5: PHIProfitabilityCycle

- **Target**: PHI nodes with cycles in profitability analysis
- **Rationale**: collectValuesToDemote handles PHIs but may not properly handle cycles where PHI profitability depends on itself through back edges. This could cause infinite recursion or incorrect profitability decisions.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect profitability analysis leading to missed optimizations or miscompilation.
