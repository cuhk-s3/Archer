## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: LargeShiftToMulOverflow

- **Target**: shl with large constant to mul conversion
- **Rationale**: shl x, 31 for i32 converts to mul x, 2^31 which overflows for signed multiplication but not for unsigned. Need to check signedness and overflow flags.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect overflow behavior or poison generation when converting large shifts to multiplication

### Strategy 2: FloatingPointIdentityMissing

- **Target**: fadd/fmul with identity constants
- **Rationale**: Patch only handles integer operations. fadd x, 0.0 and fmul x, 1.0 are also interchangeable but not supported, potentially missing optimization opportunities.
- **Expected Issue**: Missed vectorization opportunity for floating point identity operations

### Strategy 3: IRFlagPropagationNullptr

- **Target**: propagateIRFlags with nullptr instead of VL0
- **Rationale**: When converting interchangeable instructions, propagateIRFlags is called with nullptr instead of VL0. This may affect how flags like nuw/nsw are propagated from the original scalars.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect IR flag propagation leading to missed optimizations or incorrect semantics

### Strategy 4: DisjointOrToAddConversion

- **Target**: or disjoint with zero to add conversion
- **Rationale**: or disjoint x, 0 has specific poison semantics if both inputs have 1 in same bit (impossible with zero). Converting to add x, 0 loses disjoint semantics.
- **Expected Issue**: Loss of disjoint semantics or incorrect poison propagation

### Strategy 5: PoisonConstantOperand

- **Target**: Operations with poison constant operands
- **Rationale**: add x, poison and mul x, poison both produce poison, but conversion logic assumes constant is valid. Need to handle poison constants specially.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect handling of poison constants in interchangeable instruction detection

### Strategy 6: BitwidthReductionOverflow

- **Target**: Interchangeable instructions with minimum bitwidth reduction
- **Rationale**: When bitwidth is reduced (e.g., from i32 to i8), shl x, 1 to mul x, 2 conversion may overflow in reduced type where shift wouldn't.
- **Expected Issue**: Overflow in reduced bitwidth type when converting operations

### Strategy 7: MissingLogicalShiftSupport

- **Target**: lshr with zero shift amount
- **Rationale**: lshr x, 0 is identity like and x, -1 but lshr not in supported opcode list. Missed optimization opportunity.
- **Expected Issue**: Missed vectorization for logical shift right identity operations
