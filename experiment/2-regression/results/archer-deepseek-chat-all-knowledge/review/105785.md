## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: Poison operand propagation

- **Target**: ucmp/scmp with poison-generating operands
- **Rationale**: The patch doesn't check if ucmp/scmp operands can be poison before simplification. If constraints are derived from poison values, the intrinsic could be incorrectly simplified, similar to Issue 78621 with min/max intrinsics.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect simplification of ucmp/scmp when operands can be poison, leading to miscompilation when poison would have been unobserved.

### Strategy 2: Signed-unsigned transfer mismatch

- **Target**: ucmp with signed constraints or scmp with unsigned constraints
- **Rationale**: The constraint system has transfer logic between signed/unsigned systems. If ucmp uses unsigned predicates but the system transfers signed facts incorrectly, or vice versa, could lead to wrong simplification.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect simplification due to improper signed/unsigned fact transfer when proving ucmp/scmp relationships.

### Strategy 3: Wide integer coefficient overflow

- **Target**: ucmp/scmp with >64-bit integers
- **Rationale**: Constraint decomposition truncates >64-bit integers, causing coefficient overflow. This could lead to incorrect proofs for ucmp/scmp relationships on wide types.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect simplification of ucmp/scmp on >64-bit integers due to coefficient truncation/overflow.

### Strategy 4: Missing dominance check for uses

- **Target**: ucmp/scmp with uses not dominated by proven fact
- **Rationale**: The patch uses replaceAllUsesWith() without dominance checks. Other comparisons use replaceUsesWithIf() with proper dominance checking. This could lead to incorrect simplification for uses not dominated by the proven fact.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect simplification of ucmp/scmp uses in blocks not dominated by the context where the relationship is proven.

### Strategy 5: Overflow in constraint decomposition

- **Target**: ucmp/scmp with large constants causing overflow
- **Rationale**: Similar to Issue 140481, large constants in ucmp/scmp operands could cause overflow during linear expression decomposition, leading to incorrect constraint proofs.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect simplification due to overflow in coefficient calculations during constraint decomposition for ucmp/scmp.
