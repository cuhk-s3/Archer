## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: NonPowerOfTwoBitwidthReduction

- **Target**: Test cases with intrinsics where minimum bitwidth is not a power of 2
- **Rationale**: PowerOf2Ceil may overestimate required bits, leading to suboptimal register usage or incorrect cost estimation. Need to test with bitwidths like 12, 24, 48 bits.
- **Expected Issue**: Suboptimal vectorization or incorrect cost model decisions

### Strategy 2: AbsPoisonFlagEdgeCases

- **Target**: abs intrinsic with various poison flag values and sign bit patterns
- **Rationale**: When bitwidth is reduced for abs, the patch sets poison flag to false. Need to test cases where original value could be INT_MIN but reduced cannot, and vice versa.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect poison semantics or missed optimization opportunities

### Strategy 3: MixedExtensionTypes

- **Target**: min/max intrinsics with operands having different extension types
- **Rationale**: GetOperandSignedness may not correctly handle cases where one operand is sign-extended and another is zero-extended from narrower types.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect cast operations during vectorization leading to wrong results

### Strategy 4: ScalarOperandIndexVariants

- **Target**: Different intrinsics with scalar operands at various indices
- **Rationale**: The patch assumes isVectorIntrinsicWithScalarOpAtArg correctly identifies all scalar operands. Need to test edge cases and future intrinsics.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect argument type generation for vectorized intrinsics

### Strategy 5: CostModelDiscrepancies

- **Target**: Cases where reduced bitwidth changes instruction selection significantly
- **Rationale**: TTI cost model may not accurately reflect performance differences between original and reduced bitwidth implementations.
- **Expected Issue**: Suboptimal vectorization decisions based on inaccurate cost estimates

### Strategy 6: LargeBitwidthOverflow

- **Target**: Very large integer types (i1024, i2048) being reduced
- **Rationale**: PowerOf2Ceil and other calculations may overflow or produce invalid types for very large bitwidths.
- **Expected Issue**: Compiler crash or invalid IR generation

### Strategy 7: MultiUseValueDemotion

- **Target**: Intrinsics where operands have multiple uses with different bitwidth requirements
- **Rationale**: When an operand to abs/smin/smax is used elsewhere, demoting its bitwidth may break other uses.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect truncation of multi-use values leading to wrong results in other computations
