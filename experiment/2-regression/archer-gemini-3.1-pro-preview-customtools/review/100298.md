## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: Incorrect Inversion Logic in computeKnownBitsFromCond

- **Target**: A select instruction where the condition is a logical AND or OR, and the false arm (which uses the inverted condition) can be simplified based on the known bits of the condition.
- **Rationale**: The patch introduces a bug in `computeKnownBitsFromCond` where the logic for handling inverted conditions is incorrect. Specifically, when `Invert` is true, the condition `match(Cond, m_LogicalOr(m_Value(), m_Value()))` is used to determine whether to union or intersect the known bits. However, by De Morgan's laws, the negation of a logical AND is a logical OR, and the negation of a logical OR is a logical AND. Therefore, if the original condition is an AND, its inversion is an OR, and we should union the known bits. If the original condition is an OR, its inversion is an AND, and we should intersect the known bits. The current code does the opposite: `Invert ? match(Cond, m_LogicalOr(...)) : match(Cond, m_LogicalAnd(...))`. If `Cond` is an AND and `Invert` is true, `match(Cond, m_LogicalOr)` is false, so it intersects the known bits instead of unioning them. This leads to overly aggressive and incorrect known bits being computed for the inverted condition.
- **Expected Issue**: The select instruction will be incorrectly simplified to a constant, leading to a miscompilation where the false arm is evaluated with the assumption that the condition is true, or vice versa.

### Analysis

The proposed strategy "Incorrect Inversion Logic in computeKnownBitsFromCond" was based on a misunderstanding of how `KnownBits::unionWith` and `KnownBits::intersectWith` work.
In `KnownBits`, `unionWith` takes the bitwise OR of the `Zero` and `One` masks, which means it combines the known bits (if a bit is known in either LHS or RHS, it is known in the result). This corresponds to the logical AND of the conditions (both conditions are true, so we have the information from both).
`intersectWith` takes the bitwise AND of the `Zero` and `One` masks, which means it intersects the known bits (a bit is known only if it is known in both LHS and RHS). This corresponds to the logical OR of the conditions (either condition is true, so we only have the information common to both).
The code in `computeKnownBitsFromCond` correctly uses `unionWith` when the inverted condition is an AND (i.e., the original condition was an OR), and `intersectWith` when the inverted condition is an OR (ie., the original condition was an AND).
Therefore, the logic in the patch is actually correct, and the proposed bug does not exist. I will report that no bug was found.
