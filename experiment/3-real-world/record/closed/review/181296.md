## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: Truncated Step Becomes Zero

- **Target**: A loop with an induction variable whose step becomes zero when truncated to the exit count's type (e.g., `i32` IV with step 256, and `i8` exit count).
- **Rationale**: When the step of the induction variable is a multiple of `2^N` (where `N` is the bitwidth of the truncated type), truncating the step yields zero. `ScalarEvolution::getAddRecExpr` simplifies `{Start, +, 0}` to `Start`, which is not a `SCEVAddRecExpr`.
- **Expected Issue**: Without the fix, `cast<SCEVAddRecExpr>` will crash because the truncated expression is a `SCEVConstant` or `SCEVUnknown`, not a `SCEVAddRecExpr`.

### Strategy 2: Deep SCEV Expression Exceeding MaxCastDepth

- **Target**: A loop with a deeply nested induction variable or complex start value that causes `getTruncateExpr` to exceed `MaxCastDepth`.
- **Rationale**: `ScalarEvolution::getTruncateExpr` has a `MaxCastDepth` limit. If the SCEV expression is deeply nested, `getTruncateExpr` bails out of recursive evaluation and returns a `SCEVTruncateExpr` wrapping the original `SCEVAddRecExpr`.
- **Expected Issue**: Without the fix, `cast<SCEVAddRecExpr>` will crash because the returned expression is a `SCEVTruncateExpr`, not a `SCEVAddRecExpr`.
