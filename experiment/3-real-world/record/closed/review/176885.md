## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: Test with multiple uses and conflicting demanded masks

- **Target**: Instructions with multiple uses where different uses demand different FP classes.
- **Rationale**: The fix applies `Known.knownNot(~DemandedMask)` at the beginning of `SimplifyDemandedUseFPClass` and `SimplifyMultipleUseDemandedFPClass`. For multiple uses, the demanded mask might be different for different uses. If the known classes are updated based on one use's demanded mask and then leaked or used incorrectly for another use, it could lead to incorrect simplifications.
- **Expected Issue**: The optimization might incorrectly fold an instruction to a constant or poison, or propagate incorrect known classes, leading to a miscompilation.

### Strategy 2: Test with fast-math flags that restrict classes

- **Target**: Instructions with fast-math flags like `nnan` or `ninf` and multiple uses or conditional execution.
- **Rationale**: The fix adjusts the demanded mask based on fast-math flags (e.g., `nnan`, `ninf`) before applying `Known.knownNot(~DemandedMask)`. If the fast-math flags are not valid for all execution paths or uses, this could lead to overly aggressive simplifications.
- **Expected Issue**: The optimization might incorrectly fold an instruction to a constant or poison, or propagate incorrect known classes, leading to a miscompilation.

### Strategy 3: Test with `undef` or `poison` operands

- **Target**: Instructions with `undef` or `poison` operands, or where the demanded mask and known classes intersect to `fcNone`.
- **Rationale**: The fix checks if `Known.KnownFPClasses == fcNone` and replaces the use with `poison` if it is. If the known classes are incorrectly computed as `fcNone` due to the demanded mask filtering, it might incorrectly replace a valid value with `poison`.
- **Expected Issue**: The optimization might incorrectly fold an instruction to a constant or poison, or propagate incorrect known classes, leading to a miscompilation.

### Strategy 4: Test with `select` instructions and different demanded masks for arms

- **Target**: `select` instructions where the true and false arms have different known FP classes and the demanded mask filters out some of them.
- **Rationale**: The fix applies `Known.knownNot(~DemandedMask)` in `SimplifyMultipleUseDemandedFPClass` for `select` instructions. If the demanded mask is different for the true and false arms, or if the condition is `undef`/`poison`, the known classes might be incorrectly computed.
- **Expected Issue**: The optimization might incorrectly fold an instruction to a constant or poison, or propagate incorrect known classes, leading to a miscompilation.

### Strategy 5: Test with `fneg` and `fabs` instructions

- **Target**: `fneg` and `fabs` instructions with specific demanded masks that filter out positive or negative classes.
- **Rationale**: The fix applies `Known.knownNot(~DemandedMask)` in `SimplifyDemandedUseFPClass` and `SimplifyMultipleUseDemandedFPClass` for `fneg` and `fabs` instructions. These instructions modify the sign bit, and the demanded mask filtering might interact incorrectly with the sign bit tracking in `KnownFPClass`.
- **Expected Issue**: The optimization might incorrectly fold an instruction to a constant or poison, or propagate incorrect known classes, leading to a miscompilation.
