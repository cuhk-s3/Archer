## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: Oversized shift-amount constant stress

- **Target**: Mutate/add minmax tests to use very wide integers (e.g. i128/i256) with `shl nuw` by a huge constant (e.g. 2^80), then feed into `llvm.umax/llvm.umin` with constant operand.
- **Rationale**: The new fold unconditionally does `uint64_t K = ShiftAmountConst->getZExtValue();`. APInt requires the value to fit in 64 bits. For integer widths >64, legal constants can exceed 64-bit range and trigger an assert in debug builds.
- **Expected Issue**: InstCombine crashes/asserts while folding due to APInt::getZExtValue assertion (`Too many bits for uint64_t`) when shift amount constant does not fit in 64 bits (or scalable-vector splat with huge value).

### Strategy 2: Poison/undef vector-splat boundary around m_APInt

- **Target**: Mutate vector minmax tests to include splat/non-splat constants with poison lanes in either the minmax constant operand or shift amount operand, verifying no invalid fold and no crash.
- **Rationale**: `m_APInt` forbids poison for vector splats by default. That is conservative, but this area is historically fragile for poison/undef. The new fold should not accidentally transform poison-sensitive forms or regress expected no-transform behavior.
- **Expected Issue**: Potential miscompile if the fold fires with poison-contaminated vector constants, or missed fold if poison handling is inconsistent with intended semantics.

### Strategy 3: Shift-boundary equivalence checks (K=0 and K=bitwidth)

- **Target**: Mutate/add scalar and vector tests with `shl nuw` by 0 and by exactly element bitwidth (constant), combined with `umax/umin` and trailing-zero-satisfying constants.
- **Rationale**: The rule is algebraically trivial at K=0 and delicate at K=bitwidth because `shl` is poison for dynamic/statical >= bw, while APInt right-shift by full width is defined in helper math. Need to ensure transform does not appear in semantically invalid contexts or alter poison behavior.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect transform on boundary K values (0 and bitwidth) could produce wrong canonical IR or trigger invalid shift assumptions in follow-up combines.

### Strategy 4: Non-splat fixed-vector lane heterogeneity

- **Target**: Create fixed-vector tests where one lane satisfies trailing-zero/K precondition and another does not, and with non-splat shift amounts. Verify fold does not trigger incorrectly and output remains stable.
- **Rationale**: The fold matches APInt splats only. Heterogeneous lane constants should not match. This area can regress via matcher changes or canonicalization interactions.
- **Expected Issue**: Wrong-code if non-splat/per-lane shift or per-lane constants are accidentally treated as splats; or missed opportunities/instability due to partial matching assumptions.

### Strategy 5: Multi-use shifted operand with mixed consumers

- **Target**: Mutate existing minmax tests so the `shl nuw` feeds both minmax and another side consumer (`call @use`, compare, or second intrinsic). Ensure no fold occurs and semantics are preserved.
- **Rationale**: The transform currently requires `BinOp->hasOneUse()`. This avoids duplicating/rewiring `shl`. Regression risk: nearby transforms may hide extra uses or re-enable this fold unsafely. Multi-use contexts are a common InstCombine pitfall.
- **Expected Issue**: Incorrect fold if one-use gating is relaxed/regressed by surrounding combines, causing transformed value duplication and potential poison propagation differences across uses.
