## Test Strategies

### Strategy 1: Signmask-add poison boundary stress

- **Target**: Mutate `llvm/test/Transforms/InstCombine/add*.ll` with `add nsw/nuw` + signmask for scalar/vector and odd widths, including `undef`/`freeze` variants.
- **Rationale**: The change strengthens plain `or` to `or disjoint` for `add` with signmask under wrapping flags. If any precondition is too weak, new poison appears.
- **Expected Issue**: InstCombine may emit `or disjoint` from `add nsw/nuw X, signmask` in edge poison/undef scenarios where overlap assumptions are accidentally violated, leading to extra poison.

### Strategy 2: ctpop fold disjoint-safety

- **Target**: Mutate `llvm/test/Transforms/InstCombine/ctpop.ll` around `ctpop_add_no_common_bits*` using weaker known-bits patterns, `undef` masks, and extra-use variants.
- **Rationale**: Patch now explicitly creates disjoint OR inside ctpop folding, making proof mistakes observable.
- **Expected Issue**: `ctpop(A)+ctpop(B)` fold may introduce unsound `or disjoint` if no-common-bits proof is overly optimistic, causing poison where old IR was defined.

### Strategy 3: Or-concat width/lane edge cases

- **Target**: Mutate `llvm/test/Transforms/InstCombine/or-concat.ll` with non-power-of-two widths and vector forms; verify `or disjoint` only when guaranteed non-overlap.
- **Rationale**: `matchOrConcat` now emits disjoint OR; correctness depends on exact half-width partitioning assumptions.
- **Expected Issue**: Concat fold may mishandle disjoint marking in unusual integer widths or vector lanes, either missing the flag or incorrectly adding it.

### Strategy 4: Disjoint propagation through OR reassociation

- **Target**: Mutate `llvm/test/Transforms/InstCombine/or-or-combine.ll` with all disjoint/non-disjoint combinations plus `undef`/`freeze` operands.
- **Rationale**: Patch changed from post-creation flag mutation to `CreateOr(..., IsDisjoint=...)`; this can alter edge behavior with simplification and mixed flags.
- **Expected Issue**: Reassociation may propagate disjoint incorrectly between inner/outer ORs after creation-time flag setting changes, introducing poison or losing opportunities.

### Strategy 5: Builder-fold interaction regression

- **Target**: Mutate `ctpop.ll`, `or-concat.ll`, and `or-or-combine.ll` to include foldable OR operands (identity/all-ones/poison-like) and verify stable, correct canonical output.
- **Rationale**: Two sites switched to builder-form disjoint creation; constant folding can bypass instruction creation and change expected IR form.
- **Expected Issue**: Builder constant-fold path with `CreateOr(..., IsDisjoint=true)` may alter canonicalization unexpectedly (flag lost/shape changed), affecting downstream combines.
